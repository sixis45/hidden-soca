If Boka is the Soča Valley's showstopper, Virje is its gentle counterpoint — a wide, soft curtain of water sliding into a pool of unreal turquoise, reached by a walk so short you'll wonder why it isn't busier.

## The walk

From the small parking area near Plužna, just outside **Bovec**, a clear path leads to the falls in around **10 minutes**, mostly flat. It's one of the few waterfalls in the valley genuinely suited to **young kids and casual walkers** — no scrambling, no exposure.

## What you'll find

Virje isn't tall or thunderous. Its appeal is the **colour and calm**: water fanning over a mossy lip into a round, jewel-green pool. That green is the valley's signature — **limestone rock flour** suspended in the water, not glacier melt.

It photographs beautifully in soft light. Early morning or an overcast day gives the most even tones; harsh midday sun blows out the water's colour.

## Practical notes

- The pool is **cold** and the site is small — admire it rather than swimming in it, to keep it pristine.
- Parking is limited; arrive early in summer.
- Combine with **Boka Waterfall** (a few minutes away on the same road) for an easy waterfall double-header out of Bovec.

## Pair it with

Virje sits right at the doorstep of **Bovec**, so it slots neatly into a morning before rafting, or as a wind-down after a bigger hike.
