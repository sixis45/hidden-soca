Three days is the sweet spot for a first visit: enough to cover the valley's three hubs — **Bovec**, **Kobarid** and **Tolmin** — and its headline spots without feeling rushed. Here's how to spend them.

## Day 1 — Bovec & the upper valley

Base in **Bovec**, the adventure end of the valley.

- Morning: **Boka Waterfall** (about **144 m, Slovenia's highest**) — roadside view or the steeper trail.
- Midday: a rafting or kayaking trip on the **Soča** (remember the **2026 river permit, ~€18–21** — confirm it's included when booking), or a gentler walk to **Virje Waterfall**.
- Afternoon: the **Velika korita** (Great Gorge) near Lepena.

## Day 2 — Kobarid, water & history

Move to **Kobarid**.

- Morning: walk to **Kozjak Waterfall** — **25–30 minutes** from the Napoleon Bridge — and stop at the bridge's emerald pools.
- Midday: the **Kobarid Museum**, then up to the **Italian Charnel House**.
- Afternoon: a calm-water **swim** near Trnovo, or drive the ridge to the **Kolovrat** outdoor WWI museum.

## Day 3 — Tolmin & the gorges

Finish in **Tolmin**.

- Morning: **Tolmin Gorge** — Devil's Bridge, the Bear's Head boulder, and the **20 °C warm spring** (a curiosity, **not** for bathing).
- Afternoon: relax by the river, or extend toward the **Walk of Peace** sites if you want more history.

## Tips

- A **car** is effectively essential; village-to-village transport is thin.
- In summer, do waterfalls and gorges **early** to beat both crowds and harsh midday light.
- The river is **cold all year** — that emerald colour is **limestone rock flour**, not glacier melt.
