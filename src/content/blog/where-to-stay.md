The valley has three main towns, strung along the river within about **20–30 minutes' drive** of each other. You can day-trip the whole valley from any of them, so this is about **vibe**, not access.

## Bovec — the adventure base

The upper-valley hub and home of **rafting, kayaking, canyoning and zip-lines**, plus winter skiing at Kanin. Closest to **Boka**, **Virje**, the **Velika korita** and the high passes (**Vršič**, **Mangart**, **Predel**).

- **Choose Bovec if:** you're here for adrenaline and the wild upper valley.
- **Trade-off:** the most seasonal and adventure-focused; quieter out of season.

## Kobarid — the central all-rounder

The valley's gastronomic and historical heart — a great food scene, the **Kobarid Museum**, the **Charnel House**, and the **Napoleon Bridge** and **Kozjak Waterfall** on the doorstep. Central enough to reach both ends easily.

- **Choose Kobarid if:** you want one base for a bit of everything — the safest first-timer pick.
- **Trade-off:** can be busier and pricier in peak summer.

## Tolmin — the quiet south

The largest town, further south, generally **cheaper and calmer**. Gateway to **Tolmin Gorge** and a good base if you value space and value over being in the thick of it. Hosts big summer music festivals at the confluence.

- **Choose Tolmin if:** you want quiet, value, and don't mind a short drive to the upper-valley sights (and you're not there during a festival, when it's anything but quiet).

## Bottom line

First trip, want everything in reach: **Kobarid**. Here to raft and roam the high country: **Bovec**. Want it cheaper and calmer: **Tolmin**.
