Every drop of that famous turquoise starts here: the **Izvir Soče**, the source of the Soča, a karst spring at about **990 m** in the **Trenta valley**, below the **2,379 m Mt Travnik**. The river bursts out of a cave pool and begins its 138 km run to the Adriatic.

## Getting there

From the parking area on the Trenta road (below the Vršič Pass), a marked path leads up toward the spring. The first part is straightforward. The **final approach is steep and secured with a steel cable** over rock — genuinely a short scramble, and **not for anyone uneasy with heights or exposure**.

If that's not your thing, stop at the **lower viewpoint**: you still see the emerald pool and the river's first cascade, without the cabled section.

## Why it's worth it

There's something quietly powerful about standing where the whole river begins — the water already that impossible colour, straight from the rock. The colour, as everywhere in the valley, is **limestone rock flour**, not glacier melt.

## Practical notes

- Wear **proper shoes**; the cabled section is slick when wet.
- Go in **dry conditions** — rain makes the final rocks treacherous.
- It's high and in shade; bring a layer even in summer.

## Pair it with

The source is the natural start of the **Soča Trail** down to Bovec, and sits right on the **Vršič Pass** route — easy to fold into a day of high-valley driving and walking.
