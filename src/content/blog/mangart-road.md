If Vršič is the famous pass, the **Mangart road** is the secret thrill — at around **2,055 m, the highest road in Slovenia**, dead-ending at a saddle with one of the most outrageous alpine panoramas in the country.

## The drive

Branching off near **Strmec** above Bovec, the road climbs roughly **980 m over 12 km**, threading **five tunnels carved straight into the rock** and a series of tight switchbacks with sheer drops. It's narrow — barely more than a lane in places — with **passing bays**. A seasonal **toll** applies to the upper section.

This is not a road to rush. Take it slowly, yield uphill, and use the bays. Confident drivers only; nervous passengers may want to look inward on the exposed stretches.

## At the top

The **Mangart Saddle (Mangartsko sedlo)** opens onto the Julian Alps: Mt Mangart itself (**2,679 m**) above you, the Loška Koritnica valley below, and Italy's Fusine lakes to the north. It's also the launch point for the demanding hike up Mangart — strictly for experienced, equipped mountain walkers.

## When to go

Open roughly **late spring to autumn**, snow permitting. The saddle is **high and exposed** — weather turns fast, and it can be cold and cloud-wrapped even in summer. Check conditions before you commit to the climb.

## Pair it with

Do it on the same trip as the **Vršič Pass** for a double bill of Slovenia's highest roads, basing yourself in **Bovec** between the two.
