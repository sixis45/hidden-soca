**Bovec** is the adventure capital of the valley, and rafting is only the headline act. When you want something wilder — or the river's too high or fully booked — **canyoning** and **ziplining** are the local backups.

## Canyoning

Canyoning means descending a narrow gorge the fun way: **sliding down water-worn rock chutes**, jumping into plunge pools, and abseiling the odd small drop, all in a wetsuit. The side gorges around Bovec (such as **Sušec**) have beginner-friendly canyons where **no experience is needed** — a guide leads you, provides the gear, and reads the water.

It's cold, exhilarating and genuinely safe with a licensed operator. Expect a half-day, with a short walk in and a steady series of slides and jumps out.

## Ziplining

For dry-land adrenaline, **zipline parks** near Bovec send you across a side valley on a series of steel cables, some of the longest and fastest in the country. It's accessible to most reasonably fit people and a good option for mixed groups or poor river conditions.

## Booking safely

- Go with a **licensed agency** — they provide wetsuits, helmets, harnesses and guides.
- Be honest about fitness and comfort with heights and cold water.
- **Season:** roughly **late spring to early autumn**, depending on water and weather.

## Pair it with

Stack canyoning or ziplining with **rafting the Soča** (mind the **2026 river permit, ~€18–21**) for a full adventure day out of Bovec, then unwind at a safe river pool.
