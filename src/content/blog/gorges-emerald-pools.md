If the open river is the Soča's calm face, the **gorges** are where it shows its power. Here the water narrows, deepens and turns the richest emerald of all — and these are exactly the places **not** to swim.

## Velika korita — the Great Gorge

Near **Lepena**, the river carves the **Velika korita**, a canyon up to about **15 m deep and 750 m long**. Footbridges cross it, giving you the dizzying look straight down into the green. It's the signature gorge of the upper valley and an easy stop off the road.

## Mala korita and the smaller slots

Downstream there are tighter, shorter sections — the "small gorges" — where the river pinches between polished rock. Less dramatic in scale, but often quieter.

## Tolmin Gorge

At the **lowest point of Triglav National Park**, the Tolminka and Zadlaščica rivers cut twin slots crossed by the **Devil's Bridge** and watched over by the wedged **Bear's Head** boulder. There's also a **20 °C warm spring** here — milder than the river, but **not for bathing**. *(Full guide linked below.)*

## Why the water looks like that

The unreal colour is **limestone rock flour** — fine rock particles suspended in the water — **not** glacier melt. In the gorges the depth and stillness concentrate the effect.

## Safety

- **Stay out of the water in gorges.** Current funnels through the narrows; it is the single most dangerous place to swim on the river.
- Paths along the rims have **drop-offs and slick rock** — keep children close and wear grippy shoes.
- Some gorge trails **close in winter** or after rockfall; check before you go.

## Pair them

String **Tolmin Gorge** and **Velika korita** together for a gorge-focused day, breaking it up with a calm-water swim somewhere safe in between.
